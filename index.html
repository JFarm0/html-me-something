Jared Farmer's Bass Guitar Influences

A brief intro...

Thank you for joining me on this quick examination of one of my passions, the bass guitar. For the sake of brevity, I will keep the discussion based around the topic of bassists who have had an impact on me and my playing.

A selection of my most influential bassists

  • Tim Commerford
  • Les Claypool
  • Justin Chancellor
  • Honorable Mentions

Tim Commerford

The individual I believe responsible for the largest portion of my foundation with the instrument is Tim Commerford of Rage Against the Machine and, to a lesser extent, Audioslave. With an agressive fingerstyle and sound, tim.com best exemplified how to incorporate a sense of funk into metal and become part of, as Spin magazine once labeled RATM, "The World's Most Dangerous Band."

Tim playing with RATM. Explicit lyrics.

Les Claypool

This eccentric gentleman has always demonstrated the limits of what bass can do. With an array of avant-garde techniques including Flamenco strumming and two-handed tapping, Les Claypool of Primus, and countless other groups, has driven a musical sound that is both eclectic and dumbfounding. Never knew I could enjoy so many songs about fishing.

Les playing with Primus. Might be a little weird.

Justin Chancellor

Another musician who has pushed the boundaries of my understanding of what a bassist can be is Justin Chancellor of TOOL. With odd time signatures, rhythms that go against the rest of the band and switching musical roles with his guitarist mid-song, Justin shows what is possible in evocative and challenging heavy music.

A sample of Justin's playing with TOOL.
